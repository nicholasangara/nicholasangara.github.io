About

I'm a senior mechanical engineering student at Villanova University, graduating in May 2025 with a concentration in solid mechanics and an aerospace minor.

My work sits at the intersection of structural analysis and fluid dynamics — using FEA and CFD to model real physical behavior and translate it into better, lighter, more reliable designs.

I'm drawn to problems where the physics is non-trivial: fatigue loading under variable spectra, turbulent separation, thermal gradients in constrained geometries. I want to be in a role where simulation tools are part of daily design decisions, not a checkbox at the end.

Outside coursework I've applied these tools to hands-on projects — some assigned, some self-initiated — that are listed below with full context on methodology and outcomes.

Airfoil Flow Analysis — NACA 0012

CFD simulation of subsonic flow over a NACA 0012 airfoil from 0° to 16° angle of attack. Analyzed lift and drag coefficients, pressure distribution, and boundary layer separation onset using ANSYS Fluent with a k-ω SST turbulence model. Results validated against published experimental data.
